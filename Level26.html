Question: Why is SSA not a congruence condition?

Goal: Construct a triangle given two sides (segments AB and BC) and the non-included angle (A). Show that there are two possible triangles.

Nice! Because there are two non-congruent triangles, SSA cannot guarantee congruence.

Now adjust the givens (by moving the blue points) to observe that sometimes there is only one triangle, and at other times there are none.
